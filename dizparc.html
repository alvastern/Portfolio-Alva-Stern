Description of the project

In this project, me and my group explored how a procurement tool can be designed with a focus on both UX and UI. There is a clear need for such a tool, as current procurement processes are not functioning sustainably. Employees often experience stress due to factors identified as design problems. In the project, my team and I based our work on assigned design requirements and research, using idea generation to develop a design proposal for a procurement tool.

What's procurement?

Procurement is a process in which companies or organizations, often public authorities, purchase goods or services. It involves announcing what they need and allowing multiple suppliers to submit offers for what they can provide. The procurement process then determines which supplier will be awarded the contract. The goal is for the organization to receive the best offer in a transparent and fair manner.

Procurement process

The procurement process varies significantly. Therefore, identifying a general procurement process was a challenge. Our goal was to understand what aspects of the process work well and what does not, in order to identify areas for improvement. To define a process, we based our work on the general model provided by the Procurement Authority, and insights from a workshop conducted during the project.

Design problems

Procurements and the procurement processes are perceived as unclear, complex, and very time-consuming. This makes procurements difficult to manage.

The lack of knowledge about procurement in the sociaty means that not all parties understand its importance, which can result in procurement being deprioritized.

Poor communication between the different parties in procurement can lead to documentation being lost and needing to be redone.

The lack of legally approved tools for collecting documentation within the public sector means that procurement documentation cannot be stored in one place.

Design process

5
1. Preparatory work: Domain knowledge, Mapping requirements

Domain knowledge
I acquired domain knowledge within the field to ensure I had sufficient understanding to deliver an appropriate solution.

Mapping requirements
The design requirements were mapped in order to gain an overview of the issues highlighted by those requirements.

5
2. Preliminary studies: Workshop, Personas

Workshop
A workshop was conducted to identify additional user needs that could be translated into design requirements for the procurement tool. The workshop provided insights into what a procurement process typically looks like and what aspects need improvement → user needs.

Personas
Personas were created based on insights from the workshop. Since the procurement tool is intended to support three different roles, this was done to identify similarities and differences between the roles. But also to develop a clearer picture of potential users and their needs.

5
3. Idea generation: Brainwriting 6-3-5, Sketches

Brainwriting 6-3-5
To generate proposals for how the procurement tool could be designed, we used a brainstorming method called Brainwriting 6-3-5. This method resulted in several sketches illustrating potential designs for the tool.

Sketches
A number of main sketches were created with the aim of including all necessary features and visualizing how they could appear in the procurement tool. We also wanted to ensure that no design requirementswere excluded.

5
4. Prototyping: Low-fidelity wireframes, High-fidelity prototype

Low-fidelity wireframes
To develop the different ideas, digital low-fidelity wireframes were created. This was done with the aim of focusing on UX and information architecture, ensuring that the tool would be easy to navigate and intuitive for users.

High-fidelity prototype
Based on our wireframes, a high-fidelity funcional prototype was developed. During this phase, the focus was UI design, where we aimed to convey a calm impression through colors, while maintaining a professional appearance.

5
5. Iteration: Prototype refinement

Iteration
Once the prototype was completed, a critique session and discussions were held to gather feedback and improve the tool’s usability and clarity. Adjustments were made to clarify elements that were unclear, ultimately led to a more refined and user-friendly prototype.

Result: Key features in the procurement tool

5
Project overview: Timelime, Activity overview, Notice board

Addresses the designproblems

  • Lack of knowledge about procurements
  • Poor communication among parties

Addresses the design requirements

  • Structure projects and tasks
  • Follow the procurement process
  • Enable communication among parties

Design motivation
This feature provides an overview of the procurement process. The purpose is to create awareness of the importance of submitting documentation on time. To provide an overview, a section including all activities and when they should be completed is included. The goal is to make it clear what needs to be done within each project. A notice board is also included with the aim of helping individuals understand the importance of procurement, and thereby encouraging them to give it higher priority.

5
Document management: Compile all documents

Addresses the design problems

  • Poor communication between parties
  • Procurement processes are difficult to manage

Addresses the design requirements

  • Document management
  • Create and assign tasks
  • Structure projects and responsibilities

Design motivation
Within the project overview, there is a section for document management where users can see documents, the responsible person, deadlines, and upload documents. The aim is to compile all documentation so that nothing is lost and therefore, needs to be redone. This feature supports communication, as users can identify who to contact with questions. The ability to comment on one’s own document provides a way to explain, for instance, why a document has not yet been submitted.

5
Meeting management: Meeting overview

Addresses the design problems

  • Poor communication among parties
  • Procurement processes are difficult to manage

Addresses the design requirements

  • Meeting management
  • Communication between parties
  • Structure projects and responsibilities

Design motivation
This feature gives users a structured overview of all meetings related to the procurement process. The goal is to ensure that everyone in the procurement can see what will be discussed, understand what documents need to be ready before the meeting, and communicate with other attendees to clarify any questions. Past meetings are also included so that meeting notes won’t get lost and are stored in the same tool as the rest of the documentation.

5
Staff management: Staff overview

Addresses the design problem

  • Poor communication among parties
  • Procurement processes are difficult to manage

Addresses the design requirements

  • Access management
  • Management of staff and project members
  • Communication between parties

Design motivation
This feature provides users with a clear overview of staff and project members. The purpose is to make it easier to communicate with others involved in the procurement process. This helps clarify questions, such as those related to documents, instead of redoing work due to a lack of communication. In this way, previously completed work is preserved, and communication is improved.

5
Archive: Compile all previous procurements

Addresses the design problems

  • Poor communication among parties
  • Procurement processes are difficult to manage

Addresses the design requirements

  • Access to documentation from previous procurements
  • Document management

Design motivation
This feature allows users to review documents from past procurements. The purpose is to reduce the amount of work required in future procurements and to ensure that no previously completed work is lost. If an archive containing documents and resources from earlier procurements is available, the same expert knowledge can be reused in cases where time constraints limit new input.

5
My page: A personalized overview page

Addresses the design problem

  • Procurement processes are difficult to manage

Addresses the design requirements

  • Ability to structure projects and tasks
  • Management of staff and project members

Design motivation
A personal page was included to give every user an individual overview of all tasks assigned to them. This is helpful if, for example, a procurement officer is involved in multiple procurements. The feature allows them to see all activities across different projects in one place, making it easier to prioritize tasks.
