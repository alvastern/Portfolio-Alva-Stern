Portfolio: Alva Stern

Strength as a Service: ELEIKO

Designstudio, April 2024, Service design

My Role: Documentation manager

Project Owner: ELEIKO

Tools: Miro

Description of the Project

In this project, me and my project group collaborated with Eleiko, where the task was to design a service where Eleikos customers would be able to rent training equipment and at the same time, provide Eleiko with knowledge about how the equipment is being used. The goal was also to explore the opportunities for a circular business model for Eleiko, where they wanted to go from a linear to circular business model and be able to control the circularity of their equipment.

Design Problems

Eleiko now have a linear business model, because of their work with sustainability, they want a circular business model.

Companies need to spend a large amount of money if they want training equipment. The only alternative is to buy equipment.

Eleiko doesn’t have control over the second hand market of their products, they can’t control the circularity.

Design Process

1. Preparatory work: Domain knowledge, desktop research, current state analysis

Domain knowledge & desktop research
I acquired domain knowledge by desktop research to ensure I had an understanding to deliver an appropriate service for the Eleiko, that could give their customers a possibility to rent training equipment and make them go from a linear to a circular business model.

Current state analysis
I conducted a current state analysis to identify where Eleiko stands today. A SWOT and sustainability analysis was conducted to understand Eleikos strengths, weaknesses and opportunities. This to identify what Eleiko needs to do to get a circular business model.

2. Research: Interviews, Compile data, Stakeholder map

Interviews
An interview guide was created to make sure we would collect the data we would need to conduct the given task. We wanted to explore the purchase process and its pain points to understand what needed to be developed, and what may make the customers consider to not get a gym. I then conducted several interviews with the relevant customers.

Compile data
The recordings of the interviews was transcribed, ensuring we captured the data we needed. We the coded our data, which allowed us to identify patterns in the customers behavior and expectations, helping me understand needs and preferences. I then visualized the data with themes and categories to make the data easier to understand.

Stakeholder map
The relevant stakeholders was identified by creating a stakeholder map. The purpose of creating this map was to undertand which actors Eleiko currently collaborated with, as well as other actors that could be important for Eleiko’s future network in a circular business model instead of a linear.

3. Current state & Concept definition: Journey maps, Concept definition methods, Service blueprint

Customer journey map - current state
Based on the data, we developed a customer journey map to visualize the customer’s experience with Eleiko's current purchasing process. The map includes a scenario, the customers experience, emotions, pain points and opportunities.

Customer journey map - future state
Once we have created two services, future customers journey maps was created. The purpose of this was to be able to visually communicate how the developed services positively would transform the purchasing process for Eleiko’s customers.

Concept definition methods
To generate concept ideas, we conducted the brainstorming method brainwriting 6-3-5. Afterwards we conducted an idea evaluation to sort some of the ideas out. Then, context scenarios were developed for the final ideas to make them more clear and turned into a concept of an service.

Service blueprint
Once our concept was finalized, a service blueprint was developed for each service. The purpose of this was to visualize Eleiko's logistics and to outline the mandatory steps that both the customers and Eleiko would need to follow for these services to work in practice.

Result: Design solutions

Strength as a Service

This concept strength as a service is a rental service that ensures Eleiko receives a steady inflow of their used products, thereby enabling a circular business model.

Eleiko will rent or lease out new equipment in the beginning to start the flow of products, then they will have the opportunity to offer different packages, new, mixed, or used products. By offering used products can attract more customers since, based on our research, many customers thinks it’s too expensive.

Value for Eleiko

  • The concept attracts new customer groups who are not willing to make large investments in a gym.

  • The concept ensures a steady inflow of products.

  • In the long term, the concept can offer various equipment packages, including used products.

EleikoCollect

EleikoCollect is a concept where companies can return Eleiko gym equipment in exchange for compensation. This supports Eleiko’s circular business model by reclaiming and reusing equipment. Customers with a business account can have their equipment valued via Eleiko’s website. After valuation, they receive an estimated value and can have their gym dismantled and, if desired, replaced with new equipment.

Customers with unused or stored equipment can use EleikoCollect without purchasing new items. The equipment is collected free of charge, and the customer receives a voucher.

Value for Eleiko

  • The concept enables Eleiko to collect equipment from customers who have purchased it.

  • The concept promotes a circular business model.

  • The concept allows Eleiko to potentially offer rental, leasing, or sales of used equipment in the future.

Corporate Account

A corporate account feature can be implemented on Eleiko's website to enable companies to gather information in one place.

By a corporate account, Eleiko can offer specific features and tools that are only available to corporate accounts, customers could view completed orders and current service agreements, receive better support and closer contact with Eleiko and report product damages to receive service for the product.

Value for Eleiko

  • Companies establish closer contact with Eleiko.

  • Companies can collect contracts, previous orders, and other information in one place.

  • Companies will have the ability to report damages to products.

  • Companies gaining better oversight of their payments.

Insights

  • Design isn’t always about details: I learned that design could be about designing concepts and services instead of just detailed interfaces.
  • Design for the project owner: I learned that I have to adapt my design decisions and solutions based on what the project owner wants.
  • Conversations instead of interviews: Service design is about creating value, therefore it’s better to have conversations rather that interviews with the stakeholders.
  • Adapt design process: I learned that the design process needs to be adapted to the project.
  • Data from interviews: I learned that it’s essential to define the data to be gathered before interviews.
  • Preparatory work & domain knowledge: I realized preparatory work and domain knowledge are important aspects to set limits to the project.
